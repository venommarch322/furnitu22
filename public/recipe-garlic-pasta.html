Creamy Garlic Butter Pasta

⏱ Prep: 10 min 🔥 Cook: 20 min ⏳ Total: 30 min 🍽 Serves: 4 📊 Calories: ~520/serving
[ Google Ad — In-Article Responsive ]

This creamy garlic butter pasta is one of the easiest and most satisfying dinners you can make on a busy weeknight. Rich, buttery, and loaded with aromatic garlic, this dish comes together in under thirty minutes using simple pantry staples. It is the kind of comfort food that everyone in the family will love, from picky toddlers to adventurous adults who appreciate bold, savory flavors.

The sauce clings beautifully to every strand of spaghetti, making each bite incredibly flavorful. Unlike jarred sauces that can taste artificial and overly processed, this homemade version uses real butter, fresh garlic, heavy cream, and quality Parmesan cheese to create a sauce that is rich, velvety, and absolutely restaurant-worthy. The best part is that you probably already have most of these ingredients in your kitchen right now.

Whether you are cooking for a romantic dinner for two, feeding a hungry family of four, or meal-prepping for the week ahead, this recipe scales easily and adapts to whatever ingredients you have on hand. Add grilled chicken for extra protein, toss in sautéed mushrooms and spinach for a vegetable boost, or keep it simple and let the garlic butter sauce be the star of the show.

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti or linguine — any long pasta works beautifully
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter — use real butter for the best flavor
  • 6 cloves garlic, finely minced — fresh garlic is non-negotiable here
  • 1 cup heavy cream — full-fat for the creamiest result
  • 1 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese — avoid pre-shredded bags
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Fresh Italian parsley for garnish
  • Red pepper flakes (optional) — for a subtle kick of heat

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Boil the pasta: Bring a large pot of generously salted water to a rolling boil. The water should taste like the sea — this is your only chance to season the pasta itself. Cook the spaghetti according to the package directions until al dente, which means it should still have a slight bite in the center. Before draining, scoop out and reserve one full cup of the starchy pasta cooking water. This liquid gold is the secret to a silky smooth sauce. Drain the pasta and set it aside.
  2. Cook the garlic: While the pasta cooks, place a large, deep skillet over medium heat. Add the butter and let it melt completely, swirling the pan occasionally. Once the butter is foaming, add the finely minced garlic. Sauté for about sixty seconds, stirring constantly, until the garlic turns fragrant and just barely golden. Be very careful not to burn it — burnt garlic turns bitter and will ruin the entire dish. If it starts to brown too quickly, immediately lower the heat.
  3. Build the sauce: Pour in the heavy cream and stir gently to combine it with the garlic butter. Let the mixture simmer on medium-low heat for two to three minutes, stirring occasionally. You will notice the sauce beginning to thicken slightly as the cream reduces and the flavors meld together beautifully. Do not let it come to a vigorous boil.
  4. Add the cheese: Lower the heat to its lowest setting. Gradually add the freshly grated Parmesan cheese to the sauce, stirring constantly in a figure-eight motion. The cheese will melt into the cream and create the most luxurious, velvety texture. Continue stirring until the sauce is completely smooth with no lumps. Season generously with salt, freshly ground black pepper, and a pinch of red pepper flakes if you enjoy a little heat.
  5. Combine pasta and sauce: Add the drained pasta directly into the skillet with the sauce. Using tongs, toss the pasta vigorously to coat every single strand. If the sauce seems too thick or is clumping, add the reserved pasta water one tablespoon at a time, tossing after each addition, until you achieve a glossy, pourable consistency that clings to the pasta without pooling at the bottom of the plate.
  6. Serve immediately: Transfer the pasta to warm serving bowls. Garnish each plate with a generous handful of freshly chopped Italian parsley and an extra shower of grated Parmesan on top. Pair with warm, crusty garlic bread and a simple arugula side salad dressed with lemon vinaigrette for a complete and elegant weeknight meal.
💡 Pro Tips:
• Always use freshly grated Parmesan — pre-shredded cheese contains anti-caking agents that prevent it from melting smoothly.
• Reserve more pasta water than you think you need. You can always add more, but you cannot take it away.
• For meal prep, store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stovetop with a splash of milk to restore the creamy texture.
• Add grilled shrimp, crispy pancetta, or sun-dried tomatoes for a variation.
