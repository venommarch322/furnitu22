Ultimate Chocolate Cake

⏱ Prep: 20 min🔥 Cook: 35 min⏳ Total: 90 min (incl. cooling)🍽 Serves: 12
[ Google Ad — In-Article ]

Every home baker needs one truly exceptional chocolate cake recipe in their repertoire, and this is it. This ultimate chocolate cake is deeply chocolatey, incredibly moist, and topped with the most luxurious silky chocolate ganache that pours over the layers and sets into a gorgeous glossy finish. It is the kind of cake that makes people close their eyes and sigh with pure happiness at the first bite.

The secret ingredient that makes this cake extraordinarily moist is hot coffee. Before you worry — the cake does not taste like coffee at all. The hot liquid blooms the cocoa powder, intensifying the chocolate flavor dramatically while adding moisture to the batter. Combined with buttermilk for tenderness, oil for moisture, and a generous amount of quality cocoa powder, this recipe produces a cake that stays moist for days and tastes better than anything from a bakery.

Cake Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 1 cup hot brewed coffee (or hot water)
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract

Ganache Frosting Ingredients

  • 12 oz semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons butter

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Prepare pans: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans, then line the bottoms with parchment paper circles for guaranteed clean release.
  2. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking soda, baking powder, and salt until thoroughly combined and no lumps remain.
  3. Add wet ingredients: Add the eggs, buttermilk, oil, and vanilla extract to the dry ingredients. Beat with an electric mixer on medium speed for 2 minutes until smooth and well combined.
  4. Add coffee: Carefully stir in the hot coffee until fully incorporated. The batter will be very thin — this is completely normal and exactly what you want. The thin batter is the secret to an incredibly moist cake.
  5. Bake: Divide the batter evenly between the two prepared pans. Bake for 30 to 35 minutes until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Let the cakes cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then turn out onto wire racks to cool completely. The cakes must be fully cooled before frosting.
  6. Make ganache: Place chocolate chips in a heatproof bowl. Heat the heavy cream in a small saucepan until it just begins to simmer, then pour it over the chocolate chips. Let it sit undisturbed for 3 minutes, then stir slowly from the center outward until completely smooth and glossy. Stir in the butter until melted and combined. Let the ganache cool for 15 to 20 minutes until it thickens to a pourable but not runny consistency.
  7. Assemble: Place one cake layer on a serving plate. Pour a generous amount of ganache over the top and spread to the edges. Place the second layer on top. Pour the remaining ganache over the cake, letting it drip down the sides naturally for a beautiful, rustic finish.
💡 Pro Tips:
• Use Dutch-process cocoa powder for the richest, most intense chocolate flavor.
• Hot coffee can be replaced with hot water if you prefer — the effect on moisture is the same.
• Let the ganache cool to the right consistency — too hot and it slides right off, too cool and it won't pour.
• This cake freezes beautifully unfrosted — wrap layers individually in plastic wrap and freeze for up to 2 months.
