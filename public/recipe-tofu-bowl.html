Crispy Tofu Rice Bowl

⏱ Prep: 10 min🔥 Cook: 15 min⏳ Total: 25 min🍽 Serves: 2
[ Google Ad — In-Article ]

This crispy tofu rice bowl is proof that plant-based eating can be incredibly satisfying, flavorful, and anything but boring. Cubes of firm tofu are coated in a light dusting of cornstarch and pan-fried until they develop the most wonderfully crispy, golden exterior while remaining soft and creamy on the inside. Piled on a bed of fluffy rice alongside fresh avocado, colorful sautéed vegetables, and a generous drizzle of teriyaki sauce, this bowl is a complete meal that checks every box.

Whether you follow a vegan or vegetarian diet, are trying to incorporate more plant-based meals into your week, or simply love delicious food regardless of dietary labels, this tofu rice bowl will not disappoint. It is quick enough for a weeknight dinner, impressive enough for guests, and endlessly customizable based on whatever vegetables and sauces you have on hand.

Ingredients

  • 1 block (14 oz) extra-firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 3 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons neutral oil (vegetable or avocado)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups cooked rice (white, brown, or jasmine)
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • 1 cup edamame, shelled
  • 1 cup broccoli florets, steamed or sautéed
  • 1/4 cup shredded carrots
  • Teriyaki sauce or soy sauce for drizzling
  • Sesame seeds and sliced green onions for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Press the tofu: Wrap the tofu block in several layers of paper towels or a clean kitchen towel. Place a heavy object on top (like a cast iron skillet) and press for at least 15 minutes to remove excess moisture. This step is crucial for achieving maximum crispiness.
  2. Coat with cornstarch: Cut the pressed tofu into bite-sized cubes. Place them in a bowl, season with salt and pepper, and toss with cornstarch until every cube is evenly coated. The cornstarch creates that irresistibly crispy shell when fried.
  3. Fry until golden: Heat oil in a large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. Add the tofu cubes in a single layer, leaving space between each piece. Do not move them for 3 to 4 minutes so a golden crust forms. Flip and cook each side until crispy and golden all over, about 10 to 12 minutes total.
  4. Prepare the vegetables: While the tofu cooks, steam or quickly sauté the broccoli and edamame. Slice the avocado and shred the carrots.
  5. Assemble the bowls: Divide the cooked rice between two bowls. Arrange the crispy tofu, avocado slices, broccoli, edamame, and shredded carrots on top in sections. Drizzle generously with teriyaki sauce and garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions.
💡 Pro Tips:
• Pressing tofu is the single most important step — skip it and your tofu will be soggy.
• For even crispier tofu, bake at 400°F for 25 minutes on a parchment-lined tray instead of pan-frying.
• Make a quick homemade teriyaki: soy sauce + brown sugar + garlic + ginger + cornstarch slurry.
• This bowl stores well — pack rice and toppings separately for next-day meal prep lunches.
