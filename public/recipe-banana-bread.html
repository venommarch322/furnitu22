Classic Homemade Banana Bread

⏱ Prep: 15 min 🔥 Cook: 60 min ⏳ Total: 75 min 🍽 Serves: 8
[ Google Ad — In-Article ]

Nothing beats the warm, comforting aroma of freshly baked banana bread wafting through your kitchen on a lazy weekend morning. This classic recipe transforms those overripe bananas sitting on your counter — the ones with brown spots that nobody wants to eat fresh — into the most incredibly moist, tender, and flavorful loaf you have ever tasted. It is a recipe that has been perfected over generations and loved by millions for good reason.

Whether you enjoy it warm straight from the oven with a generous pat of melting butter for breakfast, sliced and toasted for an afternoon snack with your favorite coffee, or packed into lunchboxes for the kids, this banana bread never disappoints. The secret to achieving the absolute best flavor and the most wonderfully moist texture is using very ripe bananas — the riper they are, the sweeter and more banana-flavored your bread will be. Those blackened bananas you were about to throw away are actually banana bread gold.

Ingredients

  • 3 large ripe bananas — the riper and spottier, the better
  • 1/3 cup melted butter (unsalted preferred)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips or chopped walnuts (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Preheat and prepare: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a standard 9×5 inch loaf pan with butter or non-stick cooking spray. You can also line the bottom with parchment paper for guaranteed easy release.
  2. Mash the bananas: In a large mixing bowl, peel the ripe bananas and mash them thoroughly with a fork until mostly smooth. A few small lumps are perfectly fine — they add wonderful pockets of banana flavor and natural moisture to the finished bread.
  3. Mix wet ingredients: Stir the melted butter into the mashed bananas until well combined. Add the sugar, beaten egg, and vanilla extract, mixing until everything is thoroughly incorporated and the batter looks smooth and slightly glossy.
  4. Add leavening: Sprinkle the baking soda and salt evenly over the mixture, then stir gently to combine. The baking soda reacts with the natural acidity of the bananas to help the bread rise beautifully in the oven.
  5. Fold in flour: Gently fold in the all-purpose flour using a spatula or wooden spoon, mixing just until no dry streaks remain. This is the most important step — over-mixing activates the gluten in the flour and will make your bread tough and dense instead of tender and cake-like. Use a light, folding motion and stop as soon as the flour disappears.
  6. Add mix-ins: If using chocolate chips or walnuts, fold them in gently now. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula.
  7. Bake: Bake for 55 to 65 minutes, or until the top is beautifully golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean or with just a few moist crumbs attached. Every oven is slightly different, so start checking at 55 minutes.
  8. Cool: Allow the bread to cool in the pan for ten minutes, then carefully turn it out onto a wire rack to cool completely before slicing. This resting period allows the crumb to set so your slices hold together perfectly.
💡 Pro Tips:
• Freeze overripe bananas in their peels — thaw before using and they will be perfectly mushy.
• For extra moisture, add 1/4 cup sour cream or Greek yogurt to the batter.
• Wrapping tightly in plastic wrap keeps it moist for up to 5 days at room temperature.
• Slice and freeze individual portions for up to 3 months for quick grab-and-go breakfasts.
