Fluffy Protein Pancakes

⏱ Prep: 10 min🔥 Cook: 10 min⏳ Total: 20 min🍽 Serves: 2
[ Google Ad — In-Article ]

Starting your morning with a stack of fluffy protein pancakes is one of the best ways to fuel your body for the day ahead. These pancakes are everything you want in a healthy breakfast — they are high in protein, naturally sweetened, incredibly fluffy, and taste absolutely indulgent despite being genuinely good for you. They are perfect for athletes, fitness enthusiasts, or anyone who simply wants a more nutritious version of the classic breakfast favorite.

Made with oat flour for complex carbohydrates and fiber, your favorite protein powder for a serious protein boost, and just a handful of other simple ingredients, these pancakes come together in under twenty minutes from start to finish. The texture is light and airy — not dense or rubbery like many protein pancake recipes turn out — thanks to the careful balance of ingredients and the right cooking technique.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup oat flour (blend rolled oats in a blender)
  • 1 scoop (30g) protein powder — vanilla or unflavored works best
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • Pinch of cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 cup milk (dairy or any plant-based alternative)
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tablespoon honey or maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Mix dry ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together the oat flour, protein powder, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt until evenly combined.
  2. Mix wet ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, egg, honey, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  3. Combine: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir gently until just combined. Small lumps are fine — do not overmix or the pancakes will be dense. Let the batter rest for 2 minutes so the oat flour absorbs the liquid.
  4. Cook: Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Lightly grease with butter or cooking spray. Pour approximately 1/4 cup of batter per pancake. Cook until bubbles form on the surface and the edges look set, about 2 to 3 minutes, then flip and cook for another 1 to 2 minutes until golden brown on both sides.
  5. Serve: Stack the pancakes on a warm plate. Top with fresh berries, sliced banana, a drizzle of maple syrup, and a dollop of Greek yogurt for extra protein.
💡 Pro Tips:
• Make your own oat flour by blending rolled oats for 30 seconds — much cheaper than buying it.
• The batter thickens as it sits; add a splash more milk if needed between batches.
• For meal prep, cook a big batch and freeze. Reheat in the toaster for quick weekday breakfasts.
• Add mashed banana or pumpkin puree to the batter for natural sweetness and extra nutrients.
